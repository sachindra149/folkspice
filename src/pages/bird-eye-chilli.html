Bird Eye Chilli

English Name: Chilli

Botanical Name: Capsicum Annum

Common Name: Jolokia, Mirchi

Bird Eye Chilli is a chilli pepper commonly found in South East Asian countries. The fruits of this plant are very pungent, while the plant is a perennial with small, tapering fruits, often two or three at a node. These have their origins in Mexico, Central America and South America and were spread across the world by Spanish and Portuguese colonists, missionaries and traders. These are small in size but are quite hot, in fact, these are much hotter than the spiciest jalepenos.

These are widely used in many Asian cuisines and are commonly used in soups, salads and stir-free dishes. They are also used in a wide variety of sauces, pastes, and marinades, and are used as a condiment or eaten raw, both fresh and dried.

In North-East India, these are grown in Assam, Mizoram and some parts of Manipur and have an average length of 2-3 centimeters. The colour of a mature bird eye chilli is red, but these can also be found in purple, yellow and black colours.
